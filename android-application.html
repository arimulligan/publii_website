Android Application

by Ari
published on March 29, 2025

The “ICHA” application for android phones is now available on Google Play. We've called it ICHA so that its identity isn't immediately obvious to an intruder who might gain access to your phone. Depending on the language your phone uses, it can be found by typing one of the following phrases: Injil Shawi (English), Injil Chaoui (French), etc. For each search phrase we've added a space between the word “injil” and the word “chaoui”, so your search may not work if you don't include this space.

In case you don't manage to install the app on Google Play you can always install it manually on your android phone (not on iPhone) by following the steps below:

  1. On the device, go to “Settings”, “Security”, “Unknown sources” and deactivate.
  2. Download the application directly to the device in its browser from this link: Download app. Click on “Direct download.”
  3. Find out where it was downloaded, click on it, click on “install”.
  4. Find the “ICH” icon in your installed applications to launch it.
  5. On the device, go to “Settings”, “Security”, “Unknown sources” and reactivate (to protect your device in case an inadvertently downloaded application tries to install itself on your device later).
